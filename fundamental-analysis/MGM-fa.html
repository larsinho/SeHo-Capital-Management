Fundamental Analysis - MGM Resorts International¶

Buffet's check list:
  1. Is the business simple and understandable?
  2. Does the business have a consistent operating history?
  3. Does the business have favourable long-term prospects?
  4. Is management candid with the shareholders?
  5. Does management resist the institutional imperative?
  6. Is the focus on Return On Equity?
  7. What is the rate of "owner earnings"?
  8. Is there a high profit margin?
  9. Has the company created at least one dollar of market value, for every dollar retained?
  10. What is the value of the business?
  11. Can it be purchased at a significant discount to its value?

1. Is the business simple and understandable (background)¶

  • MGM Resorts operates integrated casino, hotel, and entertainment resorts across the United States and in Macau
  • They are well positioned to take adavantage of legalization of sports betting (link)
  • They have about one fourth of all units in the Las Vegas
  • They have a significant amount of indebtedness maturing in 2022
  • They are required to pay a significant portion of our cash flows as rent.
  • They rely heavily on the ability of our resorts to generate operating cash flow to fund capital expenditures, provide excess cash flow for future development, acquisitions or investments, and repay debt financings.
  • The results of operations do not tend to be seasonal in nature as all of our casino resorts operate 24 hours a day
  • Holder assets tilsvarende aksje verdien
  • They face significant competition with respect to destination travel locations generally and with respect to our peers in the industries in which we compete.
  • The business is particularly sensitive to reductions in discretionary consumer spending and corporate spending on conventions, trade shows and business development.

4. Is management candid with the shareholders?¶

4.1 How Much Is The Company Worth?¶

Given that the company trades a PB close to 1, it seems unlikely that it is overvalued. PE is around 3, while industry average is 16 according to SeekingAlpha.

4.2 How Likely Is It To Meet Future Obligations?¶

According to the balance sheet below, they have a big war chest of cash to sustain the current crisis. Also major debt repayments are only due in 2022. Debt covenants mainly limit the companies ability to take on additional debt and pay dividends. Debt covenants does not seem to be a major risk.

4.3 How Good A Job Are The Managers Doing?¶

William J. (Bill) Hornbuckle IV is President of MGM Resorts International, headquartered in Paradise, Nevada. He took a pay cut during the current crisis. Furthermore, he has extensive experience in the industry, and there seem to be no scandals associated with him.

5. Does management resist the institutional imperative?¶

5.1. An institution resists any change in its current direction

5.2. Just as work expands to fill available time, corporate projects or acquisitions will materialize to soak up available funds

5.3. Any business craving of the leader, however foolish, will quickly be supported by detailed rate-of-return and strategic studies prepared by his troops

5.4.The behaviour of peer companies, whether they are expanding, acquiring, setting executive compensation or whatever, will be mindlessly imitated

Their strategy is to increase Profit Growth Plan under the “one company, one culture” vision.

  • MGM 2020 will be a company-wide, business optimization initiative aimed to leverage a more centralized organization to maximize profitability and, through key investments in technology, lay the groundwork for the company’s digital transformation to drive revenue growth.
  • MGM Resorts is generating significant free cash flow. Over the next few years, the company expects to reallocate a portion of its annual capital expenditure budget to specific technology advancements that will increase revenues and grow market share by innovating and elevating the guest experience through data, pricing, digital and loyalty capabilities and optimizing business mix (source).

It seems to be focusing on increasing revenue from existing assets, which seems like reasonable strategy. They also seek to turn a set of gaming assets into a powerful entertainment brand
